<p><strong>Overview</strong> The Diploma Programme music course provides an
appropriate foundation for further study in music at university level or
in music career pathways. It also provides an enriching and valuable course
of study for students who may pursue other careers. This course also provides
all students with the opportunity to engage in the world of music as lifelong
participants. The course includes both internal and external assessments
and the components that music students are required to study include musical
analysis and perception, performance and composition (more details can
be found under the syllabus outline).&nbsp;</p>
<p>At ACS (Independent), the IBDP Music HL course falls under the Ministry
of Education’s Music Elective Programme (MEP) so students will benefit
from the MEP as well. Read more about MEP <a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/our-programmes/mep-jc" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">here</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Aims</strong> Through these components, the course aims for students
to&nbsp;</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Develop knowledge and potential as musicians, both personally and collaboratively</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Enjoy lifelong engagement with the arts/music</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Become informed, reflective and critical practitioners in the arts/music</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Understand the dynamic and changing nature of the arts/music</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Explore and value the diversity of the arts/music across time, place and
cultures</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Express ideas with confidence and competence</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Develop perceptual and analytical skills<strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Programme Highlights&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p>The key highlights for IBDP Music HL candidates at ACS (Independent) include:&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>1) Performance Opportunities</strong> There are plenty of opportunities
to perform solo and ensemble repertoire not just in the classroom but also
in formal concert settings. Two major platforms are MOE’s biennial concert
for all MEP students across Singapore and ACS (Independent)’s very own
yearly MEP concert for our Year 1-6 MEP students.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>2) Workshops, Masterclasses and Excursions</strong> Students will
have a chance to learn more about aspects of music in a stimulating and
engaging manner at various workshops and masterclasses conducted by locally
and even internationally renowned musicians. Students will also have excursions
with each MEP class attending 3-4 concerts a year.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>3) Fee Subsidy</strong> Students who are admitted into MEP are
eligible for a fee subsidy up to a cap of $2400 per student per year. This
is applicable for all MEP students regardless of nationality. However,
it should be noted that MEP students who also qualify for other Singapore-MOE
Awards, such as the Edusave Entrance Scholarship for Independent Schools
(ESIS) are entitled to only one award i.e. either the MEP subsidy or the
ESIS.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>4) Music Elective Scholarships</strong> Up to ten scholarships
are awarded to Pre-University 1 MEP students across Singapore each year
(applicants must have obtained at least an A2 in Higher Music or Music
in their SEC G3 Music Examination). As an MES scholar, you would receive
an allowance of $1,000 per annum. The scholarship is renewed at the end
of each year subject to satisfactory performance at the end of the Pre-University
1 course.</p>
